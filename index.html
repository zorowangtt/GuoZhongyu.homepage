    Research Experience

    Currently

    Surface photochemistry.

    Source: Guo Z.Y. et al. Water Res. (2022) 231, 118236.

    Photo-transformation of organic micropollutants (OMPs) in aquatic environment

    Frequently detected OMPs threaten aquatic ecosystem and human. I have studied the photo-transformation of frequently detected OMPs (e.g., antibiotic, antidepressant, sunscreen chemical, etc.) in terms of degradation kinetics, mechanisms, products, and toxicity. Moreover, a focus is on the effects of dissolved organic matter (DOM) on photo-transformation of OMPs including radical-induced degradation reactions. I studied the kinetics, mechanisms, degradation products, toxicity, and so on.

    Modeling quantum yield of photo-production of reactive intermediates in freshwater environment

    Reactive intermediates (RIs) can be photochemically produced by DOM in water. ΦRIs is necessary for estimating steady-state concentrations of RIs in water environment, which depends on the quality of DOM that varies from water bodies. I quantified ΦRIs in reservoirs and coastal seawater in Japan and elucidated the important properties influencing ΦRIs such as watershed characteristics for reservoir (e.g., land cover types) and bulk water properties (e.g., optical properties) for both. Then, ΦRIs has been modeled by these factors using both linear regression and machine-learning (i.e., random forest) method.
